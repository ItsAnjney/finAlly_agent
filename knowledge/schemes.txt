# PM SVANidhi (Pradhan Mantri Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi)

## Overview

PM SVANidhi is a Government of India micro-credit scheme launched in 2020 to provide affordable working capital loans to street vendors. The objective is to help vendors restart or expand their businesses through formal banking channels.

---

## Target Beneficiaries

Eligible applicants include:

* Street vendors operating on or before the notified eligibility date
* Vendors with Certificate of Vending (CoV)
* Identity Card issued by Urban Local Body (ULB)
* Vendors identified through official surveys
* Vendors who obtain a Letter of Recommendation (LoR) from the ULB if not surveyed

Examples:

* Vegetable sellers
* Fruit vendors
* Tea stalls
* Food carts
* Cobblers
* Tailors
* Flower sellers
* Barbers
* Street artisans
* Hawkers

---

## Loan Amount

The scheme provides loans in stages:

| Cycle  | Maximum Loan |
| ------ | ------------ |
| First  | ₹10,000      |
| Second | ₹20,000      |
| Third  | ₹50,000      |

Borrowers become eligible for higher loan amounts after timely repayment.

---

## Interest

* Interest rate depends on the lending institution.
* Government provides an interest subsidy of **7% per annum** on timely repayments.
* Subsidy is credited directly to the beneficiary's bank account quarterly.

---

## Digital Transaction Incentive

Beneficiaries are encouraged to use digital payments.

Cashback incentives are available based on the number of eligible digital transactions every month, subject to scheme guidelines.

---

## Repayment Period

Usually:

* Up to 12 months
* EMI or monthly repayment

---

## Eligibility

Applicant should generally:

* Be a street vendor
* Have valid identity proof
* Have Aadhaar
* Have bank account
* Possess CoV/ID card/Letter of Recommendation
* Meet lender's verification requirements

---

## Required Documents

Common documents include:

* Aadhaar Card
* PAN Card (if applicable)
* Bank Account details
* Mobile number linked with Aadhaar
* Certificate of Vending
* Identity Card
* Letter of Recommendation (if required)
* Passport-size photograph

---

## Benefits

* Collateral-free loan
* Interest subsidy
* Digital payment cashback
* Opportunity for higher loan limits
* Builds credit history
* Formal financial inclusion

---

## Common Reasons for Rejection

* Applicant is not an eligible street vendor
* Document mismatch
* Incorrect Aadhaar details
* Poor verification
* Duplicate application
* Ineligible business
* Bank verification failure

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q:** Is collateral required?

A: No.

**Q:** Can existing vendors apply?

A: Yes, if eligible.

**Q:** Can loan amount increase?

A: Yes, after timely repayment.

**Q:** Is there an interest subsidy?

A: Yes, 7% on timely repayment.

# Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)

## Overview

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is a Government of India initiative launched in 2015 to provide collateral-free loans to micro and small non-corporate businesses through banks, NBFCs, MFIs, and other eligible lending institutions.

---

## Objective

Support small entrepreneurs by providing affordable credit for:

* Starting a business
* Expanding business
* Purchasing equipment
* Working capital
* Business growth

---

## Eligible Businesses

Examples include:

* Retail shops
* Manufacturing units
* Service businesses
* Food processing
* Beauty salons
* Tailoring
* Repair shops
* Transport services
* Agriculture allied activities
* Small traders
* Professionals
* Home-based businesses

---

## Loan Categories

### Shishu

Loan Amount:

Up to ₹50,000

Suitable for:

* New businesses
* Very small enterprises

---

### Kishore

Loan Amount:

Above ₹50,000 up to ₹5 lakh

Suitable for:

* Growing businesses

---

### Tarun

Loan Amount:

Above ₹5 lakh up to ₹10 lakh

Suitable for:

* Established businesses requiring expansion

---

### Tarun Plus

Loan Amount:

Above ₹10 lakh up to ₹20 lakh (for eligible borrowers who have successfully repaid previous Mudra loans and meet current scheme conditions)

Suitable for:

* Businesses ready for further expansion

---

## Collateral

Generally collateral-free, subject to lender policies and applicable guidelines.

---

## Interest Rate

No fixed interest rate.

Determined by:

* Lending institution
* Applicant's credit profile
* Business type
* Risk assessment

---

## Repayment

Typically:

* 3–7 years

May vary depending on:

* Loan amount
* Bank
* Business nature

---

## Eligible Institutions

Loans may be offered through:

* Public Sector Banks
* Private Banks
* Regional Rural Banks
* Small Finance Banks
* Cooperative Banks (where applicable)
* NBFCs
* Micro Finance Institutions

---

## Documents Required

Usually:

* Aadhaar Card
* PAN Card
* Address proof
* Business proof
* Quotation for machinery/equipment (if applicable)
* Bank statement
* Income details (if required)
* Passport photograph
* GST registration (where applicable)
* Udyam Registration (if applicable)

---

## Eligibility

Applicant should generally:

* Be an Indian citizen
* Have a viable business plan
* Operate a non-farm income-generating business (with certain allied activities also eligible)
* Meet lender's credit assessment criteria

---

## Uses of Loan

* Working capital
* Machinery purchase
* Shop setup
* Equipment purchase
* Inventory purchase
* Business expansion
* Vehicle purchase for commercial purposes
* Service business setup

---

## Common Reasons for Rejection

* Poor credit history
* Incomplete documents
* Weak business proposal
* Existing loan defaults
* Ineligible business activity
* Low repayment capacity
* Verification failure

---

## Benefits

* Collateral-free financing
* Supports MSMEs
* Encourages entrepreneurship
* Flexible repayment
* Helps build formal credit history
* Available through multiple lending institutions
